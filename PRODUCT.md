# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners across Minnesota and the upper midwest — restaurants, lawn services, retail shops, salons, contractors, dispensaries. They have 0–5 employees, limited tech comfort, and are currently running on a Facebook page, a broken GoDaddy template, or nothing at all. They're busy, price-sensitive, and skeptical of being sold to. Most of their customers search on mobile. They don't know what "custom-built" means — they know when a site looks like theirs vs. a real business.

## Product Purpose

Pine & Pixel sells trust first. The unique mechanism — build a free demo before any conversation, show it to the prospect, then offer to sell it — is the entire differentiator. The site exists to explain that mechanism, demonstrate enough craft that small business owners believe a premium site is possible at their price point, and get them to fill out the contact form. Success = demo request submitted.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, direct, pro-craft. Three words: precise, unpretentious, alive.

Voice: confident solo operator who builds things properly and says so without flinching. Not agency-formal, not startup-hype. Jacob talks like a builder who's good at their job and knows it — first-person direct, short sentences, zero fluff.

Emotional goal: the prospect should feel relief ("finally someone who just gets it") and slight excitement ("what if my business looked like this?").

## Anti-references

- Generic "digital agency" sites: glassmorphism everywhere, sans-serif hero in Inter/DM Sans, teal-and-purple gradients, "we help brands grow" copy
- Wix/GoDaddy template aesthetic: what Pine & Pixel is selling *against*
- Over-designed SaaS landing pages: dashboard mockups as hero, metric grids, six testimonial carousels
- Corporate web studios: pricing starting at $10k, "discovery phase" jargon, 6-week timelines

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The Pine & Pixel site is itself the best demo of what Pine & Pixel builds. Every animation, layout choice, and interaction is an implicit claim about what a client's site could feel like.
2. **Precision, not decoration.** Every visual element earns its place. The lime green `#84CC16` is a signal, not a skin coat. Motion is purposeful. Nothing exists to fill space.
3. **Confidence through specificity.** Vague promises ("great results") lose to concrete specifics ("5 pages, 4 days, free before you pay"). Copy and design both favor the specific.
4. **Dark without drama.** The near-black `#05080A` and lime palette are not goth or aggressive — they're the aesthetic of precision tooling. The grain, the pixel grid mark, and the Syne display type add texture without noise.
5. **Mobile is the real thing.** Most of Pine & Pixel's prospects find businesses on mobile. The site must be impeccable on a 375px screen — not a scaled-down desktop view.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Skip navigation, keyboard-navigable all interactive elements, focus rings, semantic HTML throughout, ARIA labels, prefers-reduced-motion support (all animations disabled). Color contrast 4.5:1 minimum. Touch targets 44×44px minimum.
